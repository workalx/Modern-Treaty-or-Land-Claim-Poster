Modern Treaty or Land Claim Poster

Alex Khalus / INDG - Q4

James Bay and
Northern Quebec Agreement
(1975)
What did each side promise?
The governments of Quebec and Canada promised to recognize Indigenous land rights, provide financial compensation, and grant autonomy in areas such as education, healthcare, and self-governance. The Indigenous groups promised to allow the construction of hydroelectric projects on part of their land.
What did each side give up?
The Indigenous peoples gave up full control over all their traditional territories and accepted some limitations. The government gave up the idea of proceeding with development without consulting Indigenous communities.
What did each side gain?
Indigenous groups gained financial compensation (over $200 million), guarantees of rights to hunt, fish, govern themselves, and access better education and healthcare. The government gained permission to carry out major hydroelectric projects, especially the James Bay Project.
Which Indigenous group(s) signed the modern treaty or land claim?
The Cree and Inuit of Northern Quebec.
When did negotiations take place?
From 1973 to 1975.
When was the treaty or land claim signed?
November 11, 1975.
What territory does this treaty or land claim cover?
Northern Quebec, including the James Bay region and the Hudson Bay coastline.
Whose land(s) does it include?
Lands traditionally occupied by the Cree and Inuit peoples.
Where was the agreement negotiated and signed?
In Montreal, Quebec.
Why do you think each side agreed to the terms of the agreement?
The government wanted to avoid legal battles that could delay or stop development projects. Indigenous communities gained legal recognition of their rights, compensation, and improved living conditions.
